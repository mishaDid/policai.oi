Legal Framework

To effectively protect the rights of citizens and ensure the rule of law in the country, a number of legal acts regulate the activities of the police and the rights of citizens. Some of them include:

  • Constitution of Ukraine: The fundamental law of the country, which defines the rights and freedoms of citizens, as well as the organization of state power.
  • Criminal Code of Ukraine: Regulates liability for crimes and defines punishments.
  • Law of Ukraine "On the National Police": Defines the organization, functions, and powers of the National Police.
  • Code of Administrative Offenses of Ukraine: Regulates liability for administrative violations.
  • Law of Ukraine "On Citizens' Appeals": Defines the procedure for submitting applications and complaints by citizens regarding the actions of state authorities, including the police.
  • Law of Ukraine "On Freedom of Speech": Guarantees citizens' right to information and freedom of expression.

These legal acts form the basis for the activities of law enforcement agencies, including the police, and ensure the protection of the rights and freedoms of citizens.

For more information on legislation and legal acts, please contact specialized legal services or government agencies.
